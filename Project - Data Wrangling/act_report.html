Analyzing and Visualizing WeRateDogs

WeRateDogs (@dog_rates) is a Twitter account that rates people's dogs with a humorous comment about the dog. It was started in 2015 and has received international media coverage for its popularity. 

The ratings almost always have a denominator of 10. The numerators, though, are almost always great than 10. You would see dogs with ratings of 11/10, 12/10, 13/10, etc. Because WeRateDogs believes all dogs are cute! In fact, in September 2016, a Twitter user took issue with the account's rating system, tweeting @dog_rates to tell that it didn't make any sense. @dog_rates replied with "they're good dogs Brent". The exchange became an instant hit and the quote "they're good dogs Brent" became one of the most famouss memes of 2016.

So what does rating do? Which dog breed is the most common dog in the dataset? Are there more female or male dogs? In which stages are the dogs in? What dog type has the highest average rating? These questions and more are answered in the following insights.
